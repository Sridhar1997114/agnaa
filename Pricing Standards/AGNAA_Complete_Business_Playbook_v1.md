# PART 0 — THE FOUNDING PHILOSOPHY

## 0.1 The Dual-Market Positioning System
Agnaa must occupy the "confident middle" — not cheap, not luxury.
This is called the **Zara Principle** in positioning science:
- Zara is affordable enough for middle income to reach
- Aspirational enough for affluent people to not be embarrassed
- The brand never explains its price. The brand *shows* its quality.

**Agnaa Translation:**
| What affordable clients feel | What elite clients feel |
|---|---|
| "This feels like a proper company" | "This person is sharp and doesn't waste my time" |
| "They gave me a professional quote within hours" | "No unnecessary back and forth — they just deliver" |
| "They have systems — I won't be cheated" | "They work like a European studio but understand my India context" |
| "They are expensive but worth it" | "They are not charging me like a massive firm — smart choice" |

**The trigger words that must appear in every touchpoint:**
- Precision / Clarity / Crisp (elite signal)
- Transparent / Structured / No hidden costs (affordable reassurance)
- Studio (not agency, not freelancer)
- Investment (never "cost")

1. **Cold DM on Instagram** — They saw your work

The response does three things in exactly this order:
1. **Acknowledge** — Mirror their energy

## ACT 2 — FIRST CALL (Discovery)

### 2.1 Call Objective
The call is NOT a sales call. It is a **diagnosis call**.
You are the doctor. They are describing symptoms. You decide treatment.
Your energy: calm, curious, precise. Not eager. Not selling.

### 2.2 Call Structure — 20 Minutes Maximum

**Minutes 0–2: Anchor the frame**
```
"Before we start — I want to understand your project properly so I can be
honest about whether Agnaa is the right fit. So I'll ask a few questions
and then we can discuss next steps. That okay?"
```
(This positions you as selective, not desperate.)

**Minutes 2–12: The 6 Discovery Questions**
Ask in this exact order. Do not rush. Take notes.

1. "Tell me about the project — size, type, location."
2. "What stage are you at — is the land/space already finalized?"
3. "Have you worked with an architect or designer before? How was that experience?"
4. "What's your target timeline for completion of the design phase?"
5. "Do you have a rough investment range in mind for the design?"
   *(If they say no: "That's fine — I'll give you three options after I understand the scope.")*
6. "What's the most important thing the design must achieve for you?"

**Minutes 12–18: The close**
```
"Based on what you've shared, this is a project Agnaa can handle well.
Here's what I suggest — let me put together a structured brief and three
options for you. I'll have that ready within 24 hours. Is email or
WhatsApp better for you?"
```

**Minutes 18–20: Exit**
```
"One last thing — when you receive the quote, there's a 7-day validity.
After that I'll need to re-evaluate based on my schedule. So review it
and let me know. Looking forward to working on this."
```

**RULE:** Never extend a call beyond 25 minutes. Long calls = no boundary signal.

### 2.3 Red Flag Signals on the Call
If you hear any of these, flag internally and price 20% higher:
- "Can you just give me a rough idea of cost right now?"
- "The last architect/designer didn't understand me"
- "We need this very urgently"
- "Can you do a quick design first and we'll see?"
- "My cousin also does design, I want to compare"

If you hear 3+ red flags, decline politely:
```
"Based on what you've described, I don't think Agnaa would be the best
fit for this project at this stage. I'd suggest [alternative action].
Wish you the best with it."
```

## ACT 3 — FIRST MEETING (Site Visit or Office Meeting)

### 3.1 Meeting Preparation (Your Side)
Before the meeting, always prepare:
- [ ] 2-3 reference images relevant to their project type
- [ ] A printed (or digital) Agnaa credentials sheet (1 page)
- [ ] A blank brief template — fill it together with the client
- [ ] A phone/tablet to show portfolio examples live

### 3.2 The Meeting Opening Script
```
"I've done a little homework on your project type.
Before I show you anything, I want to make sure I understand your vision
clearly — because design only works when it solves the right problem.

So let me ask — [open with Question 6 from discovery call, revisited].
What does this space need to *feel* like?"
```
(Start with their emotion, not your portfolio.)

### 3.3 Meeting Agenda (45–60 Minutes)
1. Brief review — confirm all discovery call data (5 min)
2. Site walkthrough or drawings review — you observe, ask, listen (15 min)
3. Reference images — show 2–3, gauge their reaction (10 min)
4. Explain Agnaa's process briefly — stages, communication style (10 min)
5. Expectations alignment — timeline, revisions, communication (10 min)
6. Close — "I'll have a structured proposal ready in 24 hours" (5 min)

### 3.4 What You Never Say in a Meeting
- ❌ "This will be easy"
- ❌ "We can do it for cheaper if..."
- ❌ "Let me know what budget you have and I'll work within it"
- ❌ "Don't worry, we'll figure it out"
- ❌ Any exact price before the formal quote

### 3.5 Site Visit Billing Rule
**Any site visit beyond the first is billable.**
First visit = included in project fee (booked after quote acceptance).
Additional visits = ₹2,000–₹5,000 per visit (scaled by project size).
This must be in the agreement. State it verbally in the meeting:
```
"I include one site visit in the project scope. If additional visits are
needed during execution — those are billed separately at a fixed rate.
This keeps the project economical for both of us."
```

### 6.2 The MOU (For Larger Projects / Institutional Clients)

An MOU is used when:
- Project value > ₹1,00,000
- Client is an institution (school, company, trust)
- Multi-phase project with unclear full scope

MOU covers the same as above plus:
- Confidentiality clause (neither party shares project details publicly without consent)
- Dispute resolution (Hyderabad jurisdiction, mediation before litigation)
- Force majeure (delays due to events outside both parties' control)
- Phasing clause (how subsequent phases are priced — never at same rate as Phase 1)

### 6.3 The Turnkey Agreement (For Execution Projects)
Used when Agnaa manages contractors + vendors:
Additional clauses:
- Markup disclosure (Agnaa charges 10–15% project management fee on contractor bills)
- Client cannot directly negotiate with Agnaa-appointed vendors (protocol breach)

## ACT 7 — EXECUTION AND THE BREATHING SPACE LAW

### 7.1 The Breathing Space System
You must have protected creative time. This is not a luxury — it is the
product quality guarantee.

**The Agnaa Working Rhythm (Solo Operator):**
- Maximum simultaneous active projects: **3**
- Each project has an assigned "delivery window" (not a fixed daily deadline)
- Clients communicate via WhatsApp/email only (no calls during execution)
- Response time commitment: 24 hours (not instant)

Communicate this at project start:
```
"During the project, I work in focused creative blocks rather than
real-time back and forth. You'll hear from me meaningfully, not
constantly — and the work will reflect that. Response time: within
24 hours on working days."
```

### 7.2 The Breathing Space Squeeze — Escalation Triggers

When a client starts compressing your breathing space, pricing escalates.
These triggers are NON-NEGOTIABLE:

| Client Behaviour | Escalation Response |
|---|---|
| Requesting status updates more than once per week | +₹2,000 "project monitoring fee" added per week |
| Requesting calls during execution phase | ₹500–₹1,000/call (billed as add-on) |
| Requesting urgency / compressed delivery | +25–40% urgency premium on remaining balance |
| Changing the brief after concept approval | New Change Order (CO) — minimum ₹5,000 |
| Adding scope verbally without formal CO | Work paused until CO is signed + advance paid |
| Missing their own deadlines (late content/inputs) | Timeline extended by 2× their delay. No pressure on Agnaa. |

### 7.3 The Urgency Premium System
If a client requests delivery faster than standard timeline:

Standard delivery timeline: [as agreed in contract]
Compressed delivery (50% of timeline): +30% on remaining balance
Same-week delivery (emergency): +50% on remaining balance
Same-day delivery (extraordinary): +100% — only if genuinely feasible

State this in the agreement. Use it without apology.
