## SECTION 2 — WATER SECURITY FIRST (Foundation Layer)

### The Vikarabad Climate Reality

- **Annual Rainfall:** 900–1,000 mm
- **Rainfall Concentration:** 80–85% in June–September (4 months only)
- **Dry Period:** October–May (8 months with zero rain)
- **Soil:** Red gravelly (30–70% of Vikarabad) — poor water retention
- **Groundwater Depth:** 13.58 m by Feb 2026, worsening annually
- **Evaporation Rate:** 8–10 mm/day in summer (350–450 L/day from pond surface)
- **Temperature Peak:** 42–45°C April–June

**Critical Insight:** Water is not the enemy. *Wasted* water is. The farm receives 4.2 million litres in monsoon — enough for entire year if properly harvested.

### Water Architecture — The Master Layer

#### Step 1: Topographic Survey \& Contour Bunding

- Identify slope \& natural drainage flow
- Install contour bunds along fall lines to stop water runoff
- Create mini-terraces; capture rainwater within farm boundaries
- **Output:** 10–15% more runoff harvested


#### Step 2: Farm Pond Design (Rainwater Harvest Tank)

- **Location:** Natural low point (gravity-fed)
- **Dimensions:** 4,356 sqft × 5–6 ft depth = 1.3–1.5 million L capacity
- **Purpose:**
    - **Year 1–2:** Rainwater storage tank (NO fish yet)
    - **Year 3+:** Seasonal aquaculture (June–December only when depth >3 ft)
- **Yield:** 11.8–13 million L/yr captured from 13,068 sqft agroforestry runoff


#### Step 3: Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting

- **5 pods:** 500 sqft roof/pod × 5 pods = 2,500 L collection
- **Total:** 2,250,000 L/monsoon stored in rooftop tanks
- **Backup:** Powers drip + domestic use March–May


#### Step 4: Drip Irrigation Infrastructure

- **Mandatory:** Entire 30% agroforestry + 10% forest via drip
- **Cost:** ₹15–25 L (farmer share after 85% PMKSY subsidy)
- **Coverage:** 79 trees (26 fruit + 53 forest)


#### Step 5: Groundwater Recharge

- Percolation pits in pond bottom → water sinks to 13.58 m depth
- Target: +1–2 m GW rise by Oct 2026
