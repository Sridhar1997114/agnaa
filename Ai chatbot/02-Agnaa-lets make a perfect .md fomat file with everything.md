## 4. G+5 Building Rules – Design Constraints

> All numbers here are conceptual anchors; the exact values must come from the latest Telangana building rules and local authority.

- Height  
  - G+5 ≈ 18–20 m total height including parapet.  
  - New rules treat 21 m and above as high-rise; staying under this matters.

- Road width (master variable)  
  - Maximum floors depend strongly on abutting road width.  
  - If road width is insufficient, G+5 may not be permissible at all without special relaxations.

- Setbacks  
  - Low-rise: typically around 2 m all-around minimum.  
  - Mid-rise / near-high-rise: 3 m or more, sometimes 7 m all-around minimum.  
  - Old 6 ft (≈1.83 m) setback design is under typical minimums for G+5 and must be reworked.

- Fire safety  
  - Above a certain height: fire escape stair, fire lift, hydrants/sprinklers, and Fire NOC.  
  - Adds significant cost and must be designed from day one.

- Structure and parking  
  - Structural design by licensed engineer, soil test mandatory.  
  - Parking norm: at least one car park per unit (2BHK) or more; stilt/basement mandatory.

## 5. TDR – Transferable Development Rights

- Concept  
  - Government compensates land surrendered for public use (roads, lakes, etc.) with “development rights” instead of full cash.  
  - These rights are packaged as TDR certificates.

- How it works practically  
  - Landowner with TDR can sell the rights to a builder.  
  - Builder submits TDR to planning authority to gain:  
    - Extra floors beyond base permissible.  
    - Relaxations (e.g., some setbacks, coverage) within defined limits.

- For a G+5 Vikarabad project  
  - Mandatory TDR regimes generally kick in for very tall high-rises; G+5 typically sits below that.  
  - TDR may be optionally used if you want more floors or relaxations than base rules allow, subject to the latest GO.

- Proposed main vertical: **Agnaa Living Farm**.  
- Sub-products:  
  - **Agnaa TreeShare** – rent/adopt a tree.  
  - **Agnaa PlotShare** – micro-plot / community farming.  
  - **Agnaa FarmStay** – farm and ecology experiences.  
  - **Agnaa Vanam** – long-term tree and forest programs.

### 6.2 Core Idea

- Urban families “rent” a tree or micro-farm.  
- They receive produce at home and are invited to visit the farm.  
- They get emotional connection, learning, and experiences without operational headache.

### 6.3 Product Layers

#### Agnaa TreeShare (Rent a Tree)

- Each subscriber gets:  
  - A tagged tree with their name.  
  - Seasonal harvest (e.g., mangoes) shipped in batches.  
  - One or more visit days to see/harvest.  
  - Periodic photo/video updates.

- Design points:  
  - Tree age and health tracked.  
  - Yield expectations conservative, system uses surplus trees to buffer bad years.  
  - Clear communication that this is experience + food, not a guaranteed ROI product.

#### Agnaa PlotShare (Community Micro-Farming)

- Micro-plots (200–500 sq ft) managed by Agnaa staff.  
- City families “own” a share and receive a portion of harvest.

- Deliveries: weekly mixed vegetable boxes.  
- Visits: scheduled days to plant, weed, harvest, and learn.

#### Agnaa FarmStay (Agro-Tourism & Ecology)

- Day visits and later overnight stays with:  
  - Farm tours, harvesting, animal interaction.  
  - Banjara stories, local food, simple rituals (tree planting, seed balls).  
  - Seasonal festivals (harvest, monsoon opening, etc.).

#### Agnaa EcoMembership

- Annual subscription covering:  
  - Fixed quantity of seasonal produce.  
  - A certain number of visits or stays.  
  - Priority access to special events.

## 7. Vikarabad Climate & Ecology Reality

### 7.1 Climate and Soil

- Temperature: summers reach ~42–45 °C; heatwaves increasingly common.  
- Rainfall: ~900–1,000 mm/year, but 80–85% falls in 4 monsoon months.  
- Dry season: ~8 months (Oct–May) with little to no rain.  
- Soils: predominantly red gravelly soils with poor water retention; patches of black soil.  
- Groundwater: deep and declining; critical by late summer.  
- Variability: some years as low as ~600 mm, others as high as ~1,400 mm; seasons shifting and unpredictable.

**Conclusion:** water is the master variable. Everything must be designed around capturing and using water efficiently.

---

## 8. Brutal Design Reset – Old vs New Assumptions

### 8.1 Key Mistakes in Earlier Thinking

- Treated pond as reliable year-round aquaculture instead of seasonal rainwater tank.  
- Tried to grow water-hungry crops (like rice) on red soils with limited rainfall.  
- Oversized poultry and cow systems relative to water and fodder reality.  
- Underestimated labour (3–4 hours/day vs realistic 8–10 hours/day in peak).  
- Over-optimistic income projections in early years.

### 8.2 Core Corrections

- **Water first**: design rainwater harvesting, contouring, storage, and groundwater recharge before species, pods, or animals.  
- Replace rice with millets and drought-resilient crops.  
- Scale down livestock to match fodder and water.  
- Use the pond as water infrastructure first, fish later.

---

## 9. Correct Sequence for Farm Design (First-Principles)

1. **Topographic Survey**  
   - Map slopes, natural drainage lines, low points.  
   - Decide where water will be intercepted, slowed, stored, and infiltrated.

2. **Water Architecture**  
   - Contour bunds and swales on key lines.  
   - Farm pond sized and shaped for 8-month dry season support.  
   - Rooftop rainwater harvesting from all built structures.  
   - Percolation pits for groundwater recharge.

3. **Soil Preparation**  
   - Heavy mulching on red soils.  
   - Biological inputs like jeevamrut during monsoon.  
   - Plant nitrogen-fixing green fences (Gliricidia, Sesbania, etc.).

4. **Drip Irrigation Network**  
   - Drip lines before permanent trees.  
   - Where available, tap into government drip subsidies.  

5. **Species Selection (Climate-Corrected)**  
   - Only now choose trees and crops based on actual climate and water.

6. **Animal Integration**  
   - Bring in livestock only after fodder and water are reliably available.

7. **Pod Construction**  
   - Build living pods only after the land shows life and stability.

8. **Urban Connect / Revenue Products**  
   - Only once the system is stable enough to handle visitors, subscriptions, and promises.

---

## 10. Crop & Species Strategy for Vikarabad

### 10.1 Field Crops (Approx. 50% of Area)

- Replace rice with:  
  - **Jowar (sorghum)** – medium water, drought-tolerant, red soil-friendly.  
  - **Bajra (pearl millet)** – very drought-tolerant.  
  - **Ragi (finger millet)** – resilient, highly nutritious.  
  - **Foxtail millet** – quick-growing, multiple cycles.  
  - **Groundnut** – supports soil, gives oil and protein.

- Rationale:  
  - All require much less water than paddy.  
  - Work with the 900–1,000 mm rainfall and red soil limitations.  
  - Millet straw doubles as fodder—solving livestock feed in dry months.

### 10.2 Agroforestry Zone (Approx. 30%)

- Keep and expand:  
  - Guava, papaya, amla, drumstick/moringa.  
  - Nitrogen and support trees: Pongamia, Gliricidia, Sesbania.  

- Modify:  
  - Avoid banana unless a dedicated, reliable moist patch exists with drip.  
  - Citrus/lemon only with confirmed drip support.

### 10.3 Forest Zone (Approx. 10%)

- Teak, sandalwood, and other long-term timber and native species.  
- Early years require protection and some irrigation; later largely self-reliant.

---

## 11. Livestock Strategy

- **Cows**  
  - Maximum 2; focus on hardy breeds adapted to heat.  
  - Value: milk, dung, urine for bio-inputs.  
  - Support with fodder trees and millet straw.

- **Goats**  
  - 6–8 animals; extremely climate-resilient.  
  - Strict rotational browsing to prevent overgrazing.

- **Chickens**  
  - Cap at ~200–300 birds.  
  - Shade-net housing, predator protection, secure water plan.  
  - Free-range when vegetation allows; supplemental feed in dry months.

---

## 12. Water Budget Logic (Conceptual)

> Goal: ensure the design never promises more than the hydrology can support.

- Estimate annual rainfall volume on the entire farm area.  
- Design capture: pond, tanks, rooftop harvest, soil moisture via contouring.  
- Estimate needs:  
  - Trees via drip over dry months.  
  - Livestock water.  
  - Human use.  
  - Evaporation losses.  
- Target: annual harvested water ≥ 2–3× the total needs to account for variability and bad years.

- **Year 1**

- Farm vertical builds trust, story, and emotional connection to land.  
- Visitors and subscribers become future real estate and construction clients.

## 15. Design Principles to Remember

- Solve **water** first; everything else is negotiable.  
- Design with **climate honesty**; no fantasy crops or numbers.  
- Start **small and survivable**, then scale.  
- Every promise to an urban participant (TreeShare, PlotShare, FarmStay) must be backed by conservative, climate-sensitive calculations.  
- Health and focus are part of the architecture; without the architect, there is no Agnaa.
